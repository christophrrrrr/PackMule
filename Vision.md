# **PROJECT VISION**

## **Working Title**

Pack Mule 

---

# **High Concept**

A physics-based tower building game where players stack increasingly absurd objects on top of a small base while trying to prevent the tower from collapsing.

The game is not primarily about construction.

The game is about risk, greed, and creating ridiculous towers that should not be possible.

Every successful placement creates a decision:

"Do I stop now, or do I push my luck and place one more object?"

---

# **Core Fantasy**

The player starts with a tiny stack.

Over time, the tower grows into an impossible structure made of random objects such as refrigerators, pianos, traffic cones, giant ducks, safes, sofas, and other ridiculous items.

The player constantly balances risk and reward.

The goal is to create memorable stories such as:

"My tower was perfectly stable until I tried balancing a giant whale on top."

---

# **Design Pillars**

## **1\. Simple To Understand**

The objective should be immediately obvious.

Get object.  
Place object.  
Keep tower standing.

---

## **2\. Risk Creates Fun**

The game should constantly tempt players to continue.

The best moments happen when players get greedy.

---

## **3\. Physics Creates Stories**

Physics is not the goal.

Physics is the tool that creates unexpected and memorable moments.

Players should be able to tell stories about specific runs and specific collapses.

---

## **4\. Every Object Has Tradeoffs**

There are no strictly good or bad objects.

Every object should create new opportunities and new risks.

Examples:

Small objects:

* Easier placement  
* Lower impact on stability  
* Harder to gain height

Large objects:

* Harder placement  
* Greater instability  
* Can create large new platforms

Heavy objects:

* Can stabilize lower layers  
* Can destroy weak structures

Light objects:

* Easier to support  
* Less useful for creating foundations

---

## **5\. Player Agency First**

Players should mostly feel responsible for success and failure.

Randomness should create interesting situations.

Randomness should not take control away from the player.

---

# **Core Gameplay Loop**

1. Receive a random object.  
2. Receive random object modifiers.  
3. Rotate and place object.  
4. Physics settles.  
5. Gain score.  
6. Choose whether to continue.  
7. Repeat until collapse.

---

# **Object System**

Objects are selected from a large pool of ridiculous items.

Examples:

* Piano  
* Refrigerator  
* Safe  
* Traffic Cone  
* Sofa  
* Rubber Duck  
* Bathtub  
* Statue  
* Washing Machine

Objects should be visually distinct and recognizable from a distance.

---

# **Modifier System**

Objects may receive modifiers.

Examples:

Size:

* Tiny  
* Small  
* Normal  
* Large  
* Massive

Weight:

* Lightweight  
* Normal  
* Heavy  
* Extremely Heavy

Surface:

* Slippery  
* Sticky

Modifiers should create tradeoffs rather than direct upgrades.

---

# **Risk System**

The game includes a risk mechanic that encourages players to continue building.

Possible implementations:

* Wheel spin  
* Risk multiplier  
* Bonus rewards  
* Random events

The exact implementation is flexible.

The goal is to constantly ask:

"Should I stop now or keep going?"

---

# **Tone**

The game should be funny, chaotic, and highly shareable.

The player should laugh both when the tower survives and when it spectacularly collapses.

Failure should be entertaining.

---

# **Art Direction**

Stylized and readable.

Not realistic.

Objects should be immediately recognizable and funny at a glance.

The tower itself should become visually ridiculous over time.

---

# **Scope For Prototype**

Version 1 should contain:

* Singleplayer  
* One map  
* One tower base  
* Physics stacking  
* 30-50 objects  
* Object modifiers  
* Scoring system  
* Tower collapse detection

The only goal of the prototype is to answer:

"Is stacking absurd objects fun?"
